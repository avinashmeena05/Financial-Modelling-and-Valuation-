# 📊 Financial Modelling & Valuation Analysis

## Overview

Developed a comprehensive financial modelling repository focused on company valuation, investment analysis, and private equity decision-making frameworks.

The project covers end-to-end valuation workflows including financial statement forecasting, intrinsic valuation, comparable company analysis, and leveraged buyout modelling used across Investment Banking, Equity Research, and Private Equity.

---

## Models Developed

### 1. Three Statement Financial Model

Built an integrated operating model linking:

- Income Statement
- Balance Sheet
- Cash Flow Statement

Key components:

- Revenue forecasting
- Cost structure analysis
- Working capital modelling
- Depreciation schedules
- Debt & interest schedules
- Free Cash Flow estimation

The model provides a foundation for forecasting company performance and valuation analysis.

---

# 📈 ITC Limited Valuation Model

Performed fundamental valuation analysis of ITC Limited using historical financial performance and forward-looking assumptions.

## Methodology

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Valuation

Estimated intrinsic value through:

- Revenue & margin forecasting
- EBIT projections
- Tax assumptions
- Working capital adjustments
- Capital expenditure forecasting
- Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)

Calculated enterprise value using:

- Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)
- Terminal Growth Method
- Present Value of Future Cash Flows

## Relative Valuation Model

Performed comparable company valuation using market multiples.

Analyzed:

- EV/EBITDA
- EV/Sales
- P/E Ratio
- Industry valuation benchmarks

Compared company valuation against peers to evaluate relative pricing and market positioning.

## Private Equity / LBO Modelling

Developed investment evaluation framework from a private equity perspective.

Model components:

- Acquisition assumptions
- Entry valuation
- Debt financing structure
- Operating performance forecast
- Exit valuation assumptions

Evaluated investment attractiveness using:

- Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
- Multiple on Invested Capital (MOIC)
- Exit scenarios
